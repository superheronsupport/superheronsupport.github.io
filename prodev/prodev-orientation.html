Professional Development Orientation

What is your plan for Career Actualization?

Intro

The majority of your bootcamp will be devoted to working on "hard" skills such as learning how to code, version control, and managing deployments. The professional and personal development portion of the bootcamp will be quite different. It will be a deep dive into building confidence and understanding on who you are as a software developer. This will be a little different than traditional career counseling. You will identify what your goals are as a developer and how you are going to achieve them. In the local tech industry, "soft" or "people" skills are highly valued, employers are hiring for "aptitude and attitude" more so than raw technical capacity. They are fundamental to your success. They will require a lot of introspection and willingness to get out of your comfort zone for certain activities such as public speaking, networking, and team building. The pace and structure of the professional development process will be very different than the main curriculum used to teach the coding lessons. In this immersive training environment, please remember to be agile, prioritize collaboration, iterate to success, and always take time to reflect on what you are learning from the entire experience.

Types of Pro Dev Activities

Core Pro Dev Modules

Full-time bootcamps will have core professional development modules that will be conducted during the work week.

Friday Lab

Friday afternoons at the CNM STEMulus Center downtown is Professional Development Lab time for all students of Deep Dive Coding. Lab time can range from 1-3 hours depending on the activities that week. Some examples of activities are workshops, guest speakers, presentations, pitch practice, team building exercises, employer interviews, "Back to Basic" programming and other industry related events. Weekly schedule will be post in Slack under the #pro-dev-lab channel.

Community Wide Events

In addition to cohort specific activities, there will be occasional fieldtrips for all current Deep Dive Coding Bootcamps. They may include a visit to a local employer, business networking event, Demo Day, alumni events, etc. You will be informed via Slack about these as they are coming up.

Deliverables

Participation in all professional development acitivies is crucial to your cohort's success. They make up a portion of your grade and reflect our capacity to support you during and after the bootcamp. If you do well in professional development, more than likey you will do well in the tech industry.

These are the deliverable items you need to do in order to pass this portion of the bootcamp:

  1. Pro Dev Doc - Web document where you write your weekly recap reflections, add links to assests, and answers to homework for pro dev modules. Here is an example template for a pro dev doc.
  2. Resume/Portfolio - A printable pdf of an updated tech resume will be required. Online resume is a plus
  3. Updated Online Presence - You will need to research yourself and come up with a strategy for your online presence. LinkedIn is a good place to start.
  4. Coding Repository - You will need an online and open code repository so we can see you actually code
  5. Community Engagement - We will be look to see if you are getting out there, attending tech events, interacting with potential employers/clients
