Important Channels, Make sure you are part of them
  • PROGRAM-cohort-NUMBER (Your Cohort Channel is for all things related to your cohort)
  • #deepdivecoding (Central channel for announcements affecting the entire Deep Dive community)
  • #tech-news-and-events (A place to share news related to tech industry and events happening in the community)
  • #career-job-postings (Message board to put career and job opportunities for all Deep Dive participants)
  • #random (channel for random quirky items, developer humor, etc)

Setting Up My Slack Account
  • 2FA is mandatory (You are a developer now, you are responsible for security)
  • Make sure you put your full name
  • Add a link to your Online Repository (GitHub, TFS, Etc)
  • Add an image/avatar
  • You are welcome to change your username (coding related names are encouraged)
  • Review and Update Notification Settings (Try to reduce the amount of notification dings in the workspace)
  • Download and install the mobile app on your phone
  • Download and install the desktop client on your computer

Netiquette Ground Rules

Netiquette refers to a set of guidelines in online communication that help to ensure positive interactions. In this case specifically, these guidelines seek to keep this online community a positive learning environment for everyone. While there are no strict set of rules governing communication online, the following Ground Rules will be used as a set of guidelines in enable the creation of a comfortable and safe learning environment for everyone:

  1. Respect - Whether or not you agree with the opinion of another student, you should respect their opinion. When responding to another person's opinion, provide clear and thoughtful arguments without seeking to insult your colleagues.
  2. Thoughtfulness - When posting a message, take care to choose your words carefully. Understand that online communication tends to lack the non-verbal cues that often offer context to the intended message.
  3. Emoji - Emoticons and other cues (such as hmm... to suggest you are pondering) can be used to help offer context to messages.
  4. YELLING - Avoid typing in all capital letters. Not only does this make messages difficult to read, it also implies that you are yelling.
  5. Assumptions - Always assume that your fellow students have good intentions in their posts and/or replies. If necessary, seek further clarification of the intent or meaning of a post.
  6. Trolling - Flaming (messages or responses intended to insult or meant to be abusive) are always inappropriate.
  7. Concise - When possible, try to keep messages and posts concise and on topic and posted in the appropriate section/channel.
  8. Lingo - When using acronyms and abbreviations, make sure that they are completely spelled out the first time they are used.
  9. Convenience - When you are referring to a online asset, make sure to give the URL in its executable form (including the http://). This allows others to quickly reference the article.
  10. Security - Do not use discussions to distribute or share material which is restricted by copyright.
